Immediate Verdict: HelloBrick is superior for serious collectors and builders with sorted bins, offering 99%+ accuracy and advanced inventory tracking. Brickit remains the best choose for casual users scanning a single messy pile on the floor.

Choosing between HelloBrick and Brickit depends entirely on how you store your LEGO. While both use AI vision, their core philosophies are different. Here is the breakdown.

Brickit

The "Pile" Specialist

  • Requires bricks to be spread out on a flat surface.
  • Excellent for "magic" discovery of builds from a mess.
  • Lower accuracy on rare or complex parts.

HelloBrick

The "Collection" Pro

  • Scans pieces in bins, drawers, or hand-held.

Brickit's "all-at-once" scan is impressive but often misses small details like mold variations or rare prints. HelloBrick uses a more focused AI model that can distinguish between nearly identical parts, which is critical for valuing or documenting a collection.

If you sort your LEGO by color or size, Brickit's "dump it all out" requirement is a dealbreaker. HelloBrick allows you to scan your existing storage containers, maintaining the order of your collection while providing digital visibility into what's inside.
